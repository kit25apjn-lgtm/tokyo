About Tokyo

Tokyo is the capital and largest city of Japan, home to over 13 million people in the city proper and nearly 38 million in the greater metropolitan area — making it the most populous urban area on Earth. Sitting on Honshu island at the head of Tokyo Bay, the city blends ultra-modern skyscrapers with historic temples, traditional tea houses, and serene gardens.

Originally a small fishing village called Edo, the city grew into Japan's political centre in 1603 when Tokugawa Ieyasu established his shogunate there. It was renamed Tokyo ("Eastern Capital") in 1868 and has since rebuilt itself twice — after the 1923 Great Kanto Earthquake and the bombing of World War II — each time emerging more vibrant than before.

Why Visit?

Tokyo consistently ranks as one of the world's safest, cleanest, and most visitor-friendly cities. Whether you come for the Michelin-starred cuisine, the buzzing street fashion of Harajuku, or the quiet reverence of Senso-ji Temple at dawn, there is something here for every kind of traveller.

Top Sights & Neighbourhoods

Shibuya Crossing

Widely regarded as the busiest pedestrian crossing in the world, Shibuya Crossing sees up to 3,000 people cross simultaneously when the lights change. It sits at the heart of Shibuya, a district packed with shopping, nightlife, and the famous Hachiko statue.

Senso-ji Temple, Asakusa

Tokyo's oldest temple, founded in 645 AD, draws millions of visitors each year. The enormous Kaminarimon ("Thunder Gate") lantern marks the entrance to Nakamise shopping street, lined with stalls selling traditional crafts and snacks.

Shinjuku & Tokyo Skyline

Shinjuku is Tokyo's entertainment and business hub. The Tokyo Metropolitan Government Building offers free observation decks with sweeping views — on clear days you can spot Mount Fuji rising above the cityscape to the southwest.

Food & Drink

Tokyo has more Michelin-starred restaurants than any other city on Earth, yet equally satisfying meals can be found at a 600-yen ramen counter. Food is taken seriously at every price point.

Must-Try Dishes

  • Ramen — rich noodle soup in countless regional styles
  • Sushi & sashimi — fresh fish from Toyosu Market
  • Takoyaki — octopus-filled savoury balls from street stalls
  • Tonkatsu — deep-fried breaded pork cutlet
  • Matcha everything — ice cream, lattes, mochi, and more

Travel Tips

A little preparation goes a long way. Follow these steps before you travel:

  1. Purchase a Suica or Pasmo IC card at any train station.
  2. Download Google Maps offline for the Tokyo area.
  3. Get a pocket Wi-Fi router or local SIM at the airport.
  4. Learn a few phrases — Arigatou gozaimasu (thank you) goes a long way.
  5. Visit popular sites early in the morning to beat the crowds.
  6. Carry cash — many smaller restaurants are still cash-only.
